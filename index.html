    Project Concept & Architecture

    In supervised learning, the quality of a trained model is intimately bound to the representative quality of its training set. If the data splits suffer from sampling bias, classification decisions can shift dramatically, creating high validation variance.

    This advanced educational platform generates a synthetic, highly correlated population of 1,000 student records. It applies two divergent sampling techniques to build test splits, fits three native Javascript classifiers, and tracks accuracy metrics side-by-side to expose the superior representative power of Stratified Splits.

    Simple Random Sampling (SRSWOR)

    Draws random indexes from the general student population with equal probability. Because it acts without target strata preservation, smaller sub-groups (such as students with high absenteeism or those near the pass-fail boundary) are frequently under-represented, leading to classification bias.

    Stratified Sampling

    Divides the general student population into mutually exclusive target layers (strata: Pass vs Fail). It computes their exact ratios in the parent population and draws proportional random samples from each stratum. This preserves class ratios, keeping variance low.

    Leakage Protection Hygiene

    In order to demonstrate true performance differences, this pipeline applies strict standard standardization limits:

    • Pre-split Separation: Train and Test subsets are partitioned immediately following sample generation.
    • Feature Standardization: Mean and variance statistics are strictly extracted from the **Training split only** to prevent data leakage.
    • Hygiene validation: Testing data and user custom inferences are scaled post-hoc utilizing the training coefficients.
